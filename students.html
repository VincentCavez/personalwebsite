Advice for Prospective Students

Thank you for your interest in joining my lab! I am always looking for motivated students. Before reaching out, please follow these steps:

  1. Use a specific subject line for your email, such as "[Prospective PhD Student] Your Name" or "[Prospective MSc Student] Your Name", so I can identify your message quickly.
  2. Briefly introduce yourself — tell me about your background, your current institution, and what degree you are seeking (MSc, PhD, postdoc, internship).
  3. Reference one or two of my papers that you find interesting and explain how you would like to build upon them or extend the work in new directions.
  4. Share relevant credentials — include links to your portfolio, GitHub profile, personal website, or any relevant technical projects you have worked on.

Applying

Graduate applications go through DIRO (Departement d'informatique et de recherche operationnelle) at Universite de Montreal. Application deadlines are February 1st and September 1st for master's and PhD programs.

You can also apply through Mila (Quebec AI Institute), with a deadline of December 1st. Note that PhD candidates need prior approval from a supervisor, so please reach out to me before applying.

Undergraduate students at Universite de Montreal can contact me directly for research projects through the IFT 3150 (Projet informatique) or IFT 4055 (Projet de recherche) courses.

Studying at Universite de Montreal

About the university: Universite de Montreal is one of the top research universities in Canada. The computer science department (DIRO) is well-known for its expertise in AI, machine learning, and human-computer interaction.

About the lab: The lab is located in Pavillon Andre-Aisenstadt on the UdeM campus. All graduate students get a permanent desk in the lab space with access to shared equipment and resources.

About tuition: Tuition fees at Universite de Montreal are competitive compared to other Canadian universities. International PhD students may be eligible for tuition fee exemptions, effectively paying the same rate as Quebec residents.

About comprehensive examination: PhD students are required to pass a comprehensive exam (examen de synthese). This typically takes place within the first two years of the program.

About language: Universite de Montreal is a French-language institution, but graduate-level courses and research can be conducted in English. Knowledge of French is not required for admission to graduate programs.

About Mila: As a member of Mila, students in my lab have access to Mila's computing resources, seminars, reading groups, and the broader AI research community in Montreal.
